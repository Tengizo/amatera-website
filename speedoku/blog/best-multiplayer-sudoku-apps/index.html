Best Multiplayer Sudoku Apps in 2026

A detailed comparison of the top multiplayer sudoku apps available today, with features, game modes, and our top pick for competitive play.

February 20, 2026 6 min read

Sudoku has come a long way from newspaper puzzle pages. In 2026, the best sudoku app is no longer just a solo experience with a grid and a timer. Today's top sudoku apps let you challenge friends, compete against strangers, and climb global leaderboards -- all in real time. The rise of the multiplayer sudoku app has turned a quiet logic puzzle into an intense, social, and genuinely competitive game.

Whether you are a daily solver looking to test your skills against other humans or a complete beginner searching for the best sudoku app to learn on, there is something here for you. We tested the most popular sudoku apps 2026 has to offer and ranked them based on real gameplay experience, feature depth, and multiplayer quality. Here are our picks for the best multiplayer sudoku apps available right now.

What to Look For in a Multiplayer Sudoku App

Not all multiplayer sudoku apps are created equal. Before diving into our rankings, it helps to understand what separates a good multiplayer sudoku app from a great one. Here are the criteria we used when evaluating each app:

  • Real-time multiplayer: Can you actually play against others live, or is it just asynchronous leaderboards? True real-time competition is the gold standard for a multiplayer sudoku app.
  • Difficulty range: A great sudoku app should offer puzzles for every skill level, from beginners to advanced solvers.
  • Daily challenges: Fresh content every day keeps players coming back and builds long-term engagement.
  • Leaderboards and progression: Global rankings, XP systems, and streaks add a layer of motivation beyond the puzzle itself.
  • Offline play: Not everyone has a stable connection at all times. The best sudoku app should still work offline.
  • Free vs. paid: Is the core experience free, or locked behind a paywall? We value apps that let you play the full game without spending money.

With these criteria in mind, let's look at the top sudoku apps you can download today.

Our Top Pick

1. Speedoku -- Best Overall Multiplayer Sudoku App

Speedoku is the best multiplayer sudoku app we tested in 2026, and it is not particularly close. What sets it apart is genuine real-time multiplayer: you can race against 2 to 10 players simultaneously on the same puzzle, watching their progress as you solve. That live competitive element transforms sudoku from a solitary brain exercise into something that feels closer to an esport.

The app offers two distinct game modes. Classic Mode rewards both speed and accuracy -- you earn points for each correct cell, get time bonuses for being fast, and take penalties for mistakes (but you keep playing). Sprint Mode is a pure speed race where the first player to finish wins, but a single mistake eliminates you entirely. These two modes appeal to very different playstyles: strategic thinkers and speedrunners alike will find their home here.

Speedoku features 6 difficulty levels ranging from Beginner to Impossible, which means both newcomers and seasoned solvers will find appropriate challenges. There are daily challenges that award XP and build your streak, plus global leaderboards where you can see how you stack up against the entire player base. An XP progression system rewards consistent play and makes every session feel meaningful.

On the visual side, Speedoku includes 8 themes -- from a clean Paper and Ink look to dark Midnight and AMOLED modes, plus premium options like Ocean, Forest, Sunset, and High Contrast for accessibility. The core game is free, with optional Pro themes available as the only purchase. No ads interrupt gameplay, and no features are gated behind a subscription.

Speedoku is available on both iOS and Android, making it easy to download sudoku app on whichever platform you prefer. If you are looking for the best sudoku app that combines real competition with a polished experience, Speedoku is the clear winner among sudoku apps 2026.

2. SudokuRace

SudokuRace is a browser-based multiplayer sudoku app that focuses on 1v1 sudoku racing. If you want a quick head-to-head match without installing anything, SudokuRace delivers a straightforward experience. You join a room, get matched with an opponent, and race to finish the same puzzle first.

The simplicity is both its strength and its limitation. There is no progression system, no daily challenges, and no difficulty selection beyond what the matchmaking provides. The format is limited to two-player matches, so group play is not an option. As a browser-based platform, it also lacks the native app polish and offline capabilities that dedicated mobile apps provide. That said, SudokuRace is a solid choice for players who want occasional quick matches and prefer not to install a separate app. For a full-featured multiplayer sudoku app, however, dedicated mobile options offer significantly more depth.

3. UsDoku

UsDoku takes a community-focused approach to multiplayer sudoku. The platform is built around shared puzzle-solving experiences and a social environment where players can connect over their love of sudoku. It offers a collaborative multiplayer format that appeals to players who enjoy working together rather than competing.

UsDoku stands out for its community features and its welcoming atmosphere for newer players. However, if you are specifically looking for real-time competitive matches, the experience is more casual and community-driven than it is competitive. The feature set is lighter compared to apps that focus on ranked play, progression, and leaderboards. For players who value social interaction and cooperative play over intense competition, UsDoku is worth checking out as part of the broader multiplayer sudoku app landscape.

How We Ranked These Apps

Our ranking is based on four main categories. Multiplayer features received the most weight -- we prioritized apps with real-time competitive play that supports more than two players. User experience covers everything from interface design to load times and visual customization. Content depth includes difficulty variety, daily challenges, progression systems, and long-term replay value. Finally, accessibility accounts for platform availability, free-to-play fairness, and options for players of all skill levels.

We spent time with each app across multiple sessions, testing both casual and competitive use cases. Our goal was to evaluate these top sudoku apps the way a real user would -- not just checking features off a list, but seeing which apps kept us coming back day after day. The best sudoku app is the one you actually want to open every morning, and that is how we made our final call.
