What Is Alias?

Alias is a fast-paced party word game where one player describes a word while their teammates race to guess it. The catch? You cannot say the word itself, use any part of it, or give direct translations. Teams compete across multiple rounds, and the team that guesses the most words correctly wins. It is the kind of game that turns a quiet living room into a shouting match within minutes, and that is exactly what makes it so much fun.

The Alias - Word Clash app brings this classic party game to your phone with a twist. It supports 16 languages out of the box, comes loaded with over 50,000 words across multiple categories, and works completely offline. There is no internet connection needed, no login required, and no card decks to shuffle. Just open the app, create your teams, and start playing. The app offers two distinct game modes, Classic and Party, each with its own personality and set of rules. Whether you are hosting a dinner party, killing time on a road trip, or looking for something fun at a family gathering, Alias adapts to the occasion.

What You Need to Play

One of the best things about Alias is how little you need to get started. There are no boards to unfold, no cards to sort, and no tiny pieces to lose between couch cushions. All you need is one phone and a group of friends. The app uses a pass-and-play system, which means a single device gets handed around the group as each team takes their turn.

The ideal setup is 4 or more players split into at least two teams, though the app supports unlimited teams if you have a large group. Each team needs a minimum of two players: one to describe the words and one or more to guess. You do not need Wi-Fi, cellular data, or any other equipment. Just download the Alias app, gather your group, and you are ready to play in seconds. The entire setup process from opening the app to starting your first round takes under a minute.

Setting Up Your Game

Setting up an Alias game is straightforward, but the app gives you a surprising amount of control over how your game plays out. Here is a step-by-step walkthrough of the setup process:

Step 1: Create your teams. Tap the plus button to add teams. You can create as many teams as you want, and each team gets its own color. The app offers 85 fun team name suggestions per language, so you can either pick from those or type in your own custom name. Assign players to each team by tapping on the team and adding names.

Step 2: Choose your word packs. The app comes with 6 different word categories and 3 difficulty levels: Easy, Medium, and Hard. Easy words are everyday objects and common concepts, perfect for younger players or warm-up rounds. Medium words require a bit more creative thinking. Hard words will challenge even the most eloquent speakers in your group. You can select multiple packs and difficulty levels at once, and the app will mix them together for variety.

Step 3: Select your game mode. Choose between Classic Mode for a traditional Alias experience or Party Mode for an extra layer of hilarity with challenge cards. We will cover both modes in detail below.

Step 4: Configure your round settings. Set the round timer anywhere from 30 to 120 seconds. Shorter rounds keep the energy high, while longer rounds let players get into a rhythm. Next, choose your win condition: either a target score (10 to 200 points, where the first team to reach it wins) or a fixed number of rounds (3 to 10 rounds, with the highest score at the end winning). Finally, decide whether to enable the skip penalty, which deducts a point for every word a team skips. With the penalty on, players have to think harder instead of just swiping past difficult words.

Classic Mode Rules

Classic Mode is the core Alias experience and the best way to learn the game. The rules are simple, but the gameplay gets intense fast. Here is how a round plays out:

One player from the active team holds the phone. When they are ready, they tap to start the timer. A word appears on screen, and the describer has to get their teammates to say that exact word. The rules for describing are clear: you cannot say the word itself, any part of the word, or a direct translation in another language. Everything else is fair game. Use synonyms, descriptions, examples, gestures, sound effects, or anything creative that gets the point across.

When a teammate guesses correctly, the describer swipes right to mark it as correct and earn one point. A new word immediately appears. If a word is too difficult or the describer is stuck, they swipe left to skip it. Skipping moves to the next word instantly, but if the skip penalty is enabled, it costs the team one point. The round continues until the timer runs out.

When the timer hits zero, the round ends and the phone passes to the next team. The process repeats, team by team, round by round, until the win condition is met. The team that reaches the target score first, or has the most points after the final round, wins the game. Classic Mode rewards clear communication, quick thinking, and a strong vocabulary. It is easy to pick up but surprisingly hard to master, especially with harder word packs in the mix.

Party Mode Rules

Party Mode takes everything that makes Classic Mode great and adds a layer of chaos. The core rules are identical: describe words, swipe right for correct, swipe left to skip, race against the timer. But Party Mode introduces Challenge Cards that completely change how you have to describe each word.

At the start of each round, before the timer begins, a random Challenge Card appears on screen. This challenge sets a constraint that the describer must follow for the entire round. The challenges range from mildly tricky to utterly absurd, and that is what makes Party Mode so entertaining. Here are some examples of the 25 built-in challenges:

  • Robot Voice — Describe every word talking like a robot
  • Whisper Mode — You can only whisper your descriptions
  • Three Words Only — Each description must be exactly three words
  • One Leg — Stand on one leg while describing (fall and the round is over)
  • No Hands — Keep your hands behind your back, no gestures allowed
  • Sing It — Every description must be sung, not spoken
  • Opposite Day — Describe the opposite of the word and let your team figure it out
  • Eyes Closed — Describe with your eyes shut

The app comes with 25 challenge cards, but you can also create your own custom challenges with custom text and emoji icons. This is perfect for inside jokes, themed parties, or adding challenges that are specific to your group. You can also set the challenge frequency from every round to every 5 rounds, depending on how much chaos you want.

Party Mode is where Alias truly shines as a party game. The challenges force players out of their comfort zones and create moments that your group will be talking about for weeks. It is one thing to describe the word "elephant." It is another thing entirely to describe "elephant" while whispering and standing on one leg. The laughter that comes from watching someone struggle through a challenge is half the fun of the game.

Scoring & Winning

Alias keeps scoring simple so the focus stays on the fun. Every word your team guesses correctly earns one point. If the skip penalty is enabled, every skipped word costs one point. Your team's score is the total of correct guesses minus any skip penalties accumulated across all rounds.

There are two ways to determine a winner. With the target score setting, the first team to reach or exceed the chosen point threshold wins immediately. This creates a race dynamic where every round feels like it could be the deciding one. With the fixed rounds setting, all teams play the same number of rounds, and the team with the highest score at the end wins. This is the fairer option for groups where teams have different skill levels, since everyone gets the same number of turns.

When the game ends, the app displays a Game Over screen with a ranked leaderboard showing every team's final score. You can see each team's total correct answers, skips, and net score at a glance. It is a satisfying way to wrap up a game, especially when the final scores are close. The leaderboard also makes it easy to settle any friendly debates about who really is the best word describer in the group.

The Contest System

One of the most clever features in Alias is the contest system. It solves a problem that every word game player has experienced: the ambiguous guess. Did the teammate actually say the right word, or something close enough? Was the describer's clue technically legal, or did they accidentally say part of the word? These disputes can ruin the flow of a game, but the contest system handles them elegantly.

After each round, the app shows a review of all the words that were skipped during that round. Any player from any team can tap on a skipped word to contest it. This triggers a vote where the opposing teams decide whether the point should be awarded. If the majority votes to approve the contest, the describing team gets the point. If the majority rejects it, the skip stands. It is democracy in action, and it keeps the game fair without needing a referee.

The contest system is especially useful in close games where a single point can make the difference between winning and losing. It adds a strategic layer to the review phase and keeps every player engaged even when it is not their team's turn. Plus, the discussions that happen during contests are often some of the funniest moments in the game.

Tips for Beginners

If you are new to Alias, these tips will help you go from confused first-timer to confident competitor in no time:

  • Use synonyms and associations, not dictionary definitions. Saying "it is a large gray animal with a trunk" works much faster than trying to recite the textbook definition of "elephant." Think about what your teammates would associate with the word and use those connections.
  • Gesture when you can. Your hands are powerful communication tools. Point, mime, act things out. Your body language can convey ideas that words alone struggle with. Just make sure you are not playing the "No Hands" challenge when you do this!
  • Skip strategically. Do not waste ten seconds staring at a word you have no idea how to describe. If nothing comes to mind in the first two seconds, swipe left and move on. Even with the skip penalty enabled, getting stuck on one word is more costly than taking the one-point hit.
  • In Party Mode, commit to the challenge fully. Half-hearting a challenge is never as funny as going all in. If you are supposed to sing your descriptions, really sing them. The more you commit, the more fun everyone has, and your team will actually guess faster when your delivery is clear and confident.
  • Mix difficulty levels for the best experience. Start with Easy word packs for your first game to get the hang of the mechanics. Then mix in Medium and Hard words as your group warms up. The variety keeps every round feeling fresh and prevents the game from becoming predictable.
  • Try language mixing for multilingual groups. If your group speaks multiple languages, select word packs from different languages. The app supports 16 languages, and you can combine them in a single game. Describing a Finnish word to an English-speaking friend is a whole new kind of challenge.
  • Create custom challenges for your group. The 25 built-in challenges are great, but custom challenges that reference inside jokes or target specific friends take the game to another level. Try challenges like "Describe in the voice of [friend's name]" or "Must include a movie quote in every description."
  • Track your stats across games. Pay attention to which team members excel at describing versus guessing. Over time, you can strategically pair strong describers with strong guessers for more competitive matches.
